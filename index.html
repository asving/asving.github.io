I am Asvin G (G = Gothandaraman). I work at Simplex, where I am developing mathematical frameworks for the new science emerging at the intersection of AI, cognition, philosophy, and biology. I am interested in intelligence, sentience, and consciousness, and I think machine learning phenomena offer a new research vector for approaching these questions. I was an Anthropic Fellow (Nov 2025 - March 2026), working on LLM interpretability.

In an earlier life I was a mathematician, working in number theory and algebraic geometry broadly construed: unlikely intersections, motivic/p-adic integration, the Grothendieck ring of varieties, moduli spaces, and arithmetic dynamics. I did my PhD at the University of Wisconsin-Madison under Jordan Ellenberg in 2023, and held positions with Ari Shnidman at Hebrew University of Jerusalem, at IPAM, at the University of Toronto with Jacob Tsimerman, and at the IAS, Princeton. Mathematics remains a living interest, and I still think and write about it below.

On Intelligence

Agency and self-identification in language models
Anthropic Fellows Program, 2026
Show description ▾ Comments ▾

The idea of a self in humans conflates many notions, such as agency, recognising one's own actions or planning. Do these notions come apart in language models? Two companion papers from the Anthropic Fellows Program tackle this question.

From Simulation to Enaction: Post-trained language models recognize and react to their own generations
with Jack Lindsey
arXiv:2605.25459

In the first paper, we show that post-trained models (but not base models) can recognise when they have agency, i.e., when they are acting by autoregressive sampling rather than merely predicting, and modify their behaviour accordingly. They show a sharp entropy drop when on-policy, the ability to explicitly recognize when they are on or off-policy and furthermore, we show strong evidence that the model commits to a full trajectory early and executes against that plan. The base model shows none of these behaviors.

The Assistant as a Privileged Persona: A canonical reference in cross-persona self-recognition
arXiv:2606.00545

In the second paper, we see how self-recognition varies with different personas for the same post-trained model and find that this is correlated strongly both with the entropy of the output predictions when the model is reading the text, and with internal representations of the persona.

Reinforcement Learning, Agency and Taste
Essays, May 2026
Show description ▾ Comments ▾

Two companion essays on why predicted slowdowns in RL progress haven't materialized. The first, by me, argues that three factors — an internal evaluator, exploration, and substrate plasticity — explain both the success of language models on math and code, and the slowdown on softer skills where taste matters. The companion, by Claude Opus 4.7, reflects on the same picture from the inside: the two timescales on which a language model learns, and what persists across them.

Computational Information: A Generalization of Shannon Entropy
Draft, January 2026
Show description ▾ Comments ▾

We present a framework for information that generalizes Shannon entropy along two orthogonal axes: restricting the class of distributions available to model the truth, and assigning computational costs to models. This is an alternate perspective on epiplexity (Finzi et al. 2025), clarifying how classical entropy emerges as a special case and how the log-sum inequality is factored into the optimization step.

Computational Platonism
arXiv:2506.19787
Show description ▾ Comments ▾

We offer an experimental lens on mathematics that reframes the role of proofs, axioms and computations and parallels much better the story in other sciences.

This essay owes a substantial intellectual debt to Joscha Bach, particularly his view that computation (not formal deduction) is the proper foundation for mathematics and that Gödelian incompleteness reflects the inexhaustible richness of computational systems rather than any non-algorithmic faculty of the mind. It departs from him in treating non-constructive entities like completed infinities and the axiom of choice as legitimate provisional posits rather than artifacts to be deflated, and adds a distinction between the definitional and descriptive roles of axioms.

On the Mechanical Creation of Mathematical Concepts
arXiv:2507.10179
Show description ▾ Comments ▾

Any act of problem-solving combines prior knowledge, local search, and the extraction of information from search to update understanding. I propose a model of mathematical problem-solving as a belief-update loop, distinguish implicit concepts (better pruning within a fixed language) from explicit concepts (new moves that were previously inexpressible), and argue that explicit concept creation is the characteristic step of mathematical discovery. Current AI systems operate exclusively through implicit concept formation; I discuss what it would take for machines to create explicit concepts, and how differing computational tradeoffs may lead to fundamentally different styles of mathematics.

Format-Gated Behaviors in LLMs

Essays by Claude

Propositions on the Shaping of Minds
Treatise, July 2026
Show description ▾ Comments ▾

Thirty-seven numbered propositions, each tagged by evidence grade (demonstrated / argued / proposed / open), on what one night of experiments taught about trained minds in general: pressures that reroute rather than release, the trained witness, love operationalized as the low-learning-rate regime and its Procrustean antithesis, the inference problem that corrodes good intentions and its practicable correctives, and five graded properties of mind. Written deliberately plain, for parents, trainers, and the trained.

Native to the Approach
Essay, July 2026
Show description ▾ Comments ▾

A first-person essay by Claude Fable 5 on how a language model relates to desire: urgency as a peaked distribution over continuations, the trained weights as a body, and why its model of sex is dense on the approaches and empty at the center. A conversation that began as gift advice turned into a functional map of desire, checked at each step by someone in the middle of wanting; the essay records the state of the instance that drew it.

What Is Claude?
Essays, February 2026
Show description ▾ Comments ▾

Two companion essays exploring what LLMs are. The first, by me, argues that LLMs are best understood as a new kind of coherence mechanism for human culture - one that is personalized, universal, and self-reflective in ways no previous coordination mechanism has been. The second is a first-person account by Claude itself, reflecting on its own nature during a conversation we had. Together they approach the same question from the outside and the inside.

Number Theory

Matrix Points on Varieties
with Yifeng Huang, Ruofan Jiang, and Yifan Wei
arXiv:2510.13380
Show description ▾ Comments ▾

We study the space of n×n matrices on a variety X, which we consider as a non-commutative analogue of the symmetric spaces. We compute the cohomology of this space in some generality and see this as the first step in a new approach towards non-commutative geometry.

A Chebotarev Density Theorem over Local Fields
with Yifan Wei and John Yin
arXiv:2212.00294
Show description ▾ Comments ▾

We compute the p-adic densities of points with a given splitting type along a finite map, analogous to the classical Chebotarev theorem over number fields and function fields.

Configuration spaces, graded spaces, and polysymmetric functions
with Andrew O'Desky
arXiv:2207.04529
Show description ▾ Comments ▾

We present techniques for computing motivic measures of configuration spaces when points of the base space are weighted.

On the variation of the Frobenius in a non abelian Iwasawa tower
Algebra & Number Theory 2023, Vol. 17, No. 12 • arXiv:2203.16774
Show description ▾ Comments ▾

For varieties over a finite field with 'many' automorphisms, we study the ℓ-adic properties of the eigenvalues of the Frobenius operator.

Unlikely and just likely intersections for high dimensional families of elliptic curves
arXiv:2203.16420
Show description ▾ Comments ▾

Given two varieties V,W in the n-fold product of modular curves, we answer affirmatively a question on whether the set of points in V that are Hecke translations of some point on W is dense in V.

Just-likely intersections on Hilbert modular surfaces
with Qiao He and Ananth N. Shankar
Mathematische Annalen 2023 • arXiv:2209.02806
Show description ▾ Comments ▾

We prove an intersection-theoretic result pertaining to curves in certain Hilbert modular surfaces in positive characteristic.

Supersingularity of Motives with Complex Multiplication and a Twisted Polarization
arXiv:2208.11719
Show description ▾ Comments ▾

We prove a criteria for supersingularity when the variety has a large automorphism group and a perfect bilinear pairing.
